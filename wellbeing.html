Five Ways to Wellbeing

Improving your wellbeing can make a big difference to your mental health. These are the five evidence-based steps to improve your mental wellbeing:

  • Connect: Build connections with the people around you. Social relationships are crucial for mental wellbeing.
  • Be Active: Physical activity can boost your mood and improve your energy levels. Regular exercise helps relieve stress.
  • Take Notice: Be mindful of the world around you. Practicing mindfulness helps to relieve stress and improve concentration.
  • Keep Learning: Learning something new, whether it’s a skill or a hobby, can boost your self-esteem and provide purpose.
  • Give: Helping others and giving back to your community can increase feelings of happiness and satisfaction.

For more details, visit Mental Health Ireland's Five Ways to Wellbeing page.

Mental Health Resources for Post-Docs and PhD Students

Being a researcher can come with a unique set of challenges. It’s important to recognize and take care of your mental health. Here are some resources specifically aimed at supporting researchers and academics:

  • NUI Galway Mental Health Resources - Specialized mental health services for students and researchers.
  • Psychological Society of Ireland - Supporting mental health professionals, including services for researchers and academics.

Additional Wellbeing Tips for Researchers

As a researcher, you may often be working under pressure with deadlines, experiments, and academic goals. Here are some additional tips to ensure you stay well:

  • Set boundaries between work and personal life to avoid burnout.
  • Take regular breaks throughout your workday.
  • Develop a support system, including fellow researchers or mentors.
  • Make time for social activities to maintain a work-life balance.
  • Engage in mindfulness practices to relieve stress.
