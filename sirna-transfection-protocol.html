Key Considerations:

  • Typical time points for detecting target knockdown with lipid-mediated siRNA or microRNA mimic delivery are 24-48 hours for mRNA and 48-96 hours for protein.
  • Follow manufacturer’s recommended final concentration. Typically, we use a 10 nM final concentration of the siRNA (can be 10-50 nM). Use a matching concentration of negative control siRNA (scramble siRNA). For example, if your stock concentration is 50 µM and you want a 25 nM final concentration, this would require a 1/2000 dilution in your final transfection mixture.
  • Use low-passage cells (<50 passages). Ensure cells are healthy and greater than 90% viable before transfection.

Day 0:

One day before transfection, plate cells in a 6-well plate.

  • Trypsinize cells and resuspend the pellet in 3 mL of media without antibiotics.
  • Count the cells using a hemocytometer.
  • Seed the appropriate number of cells into a 6-well plate (this depends on your cell type and growth rate; ensure you're familiar with these parameters).
  • Add 2 mL of media without antibiotics and leave the plate overnight in the incubator.
  • Target cell density should be 30–50% confluent at the time of transfection.
  • For RNA tests, you may only need 1 or 2 wells per condition. For protein, you may need more wells.

Expected Seeding Densities:

  • JIMT1BR cells: 125,000 cells per 6-well.

Day 1:

Check that your cells are at the right level of confluence. Prepare your siRNA mastermix.

Reagents needed:

  • Opti-Mem (Low serum transfection media, tissue culture main fridge)
  • Lipofectamine 2000 (Transfection reagent, tissue culture main fridge)
  • Scramble siRNA (negative control, stock conc 50 µM)
  • siRNA (stock conc 50 µM or lower, stored at -20°C in the main lab)

Typically for one transfection, the mastermix is as follows (final volume of siRNA and Opti-MEM depends on your desired final concentration):

  • 1 µL of siRNA/scramble + 199 µL of Opti-MEM
  • 5 µL of Lipofectamine 2000 + 195 µL of Opti-MEM

For 3 wells of NT (negative control) and 3 wells of siRNA, scale up as necessary:

  1. Prepare 3x 1.5 mL Eppendorf tubes. In the first tube: Pipette 3 µL of siNT (negative control) + 597 µL of Opti-MEM.
  2. In the second tube: Pipette 15 µL of Lipofectamine + 585 µL of Opti-MEM.
  3. In the third tube: Pipette 3 µL of siRNA + 597 µL of Opti-MEM.
  4. In the fourth tube: Pipette 15 µL of Lipofectamine + 585 µL of Opti-MEM.
  5. Let this mixture sit for 5 minutes.

After 5 minutes, mix the contents of tube 1 and 2 (NT or scramble mix), and the contents of tube 3 and 4 (siRNA mix). Let the mixtures incubate for 10-20 minutes in the hood.

After the incubation, take your 6-well plate out of the incubator and remove the old media. Add 1.6 mL of Opti-MEM into each well, then pipette 400 µL of the mix to the corresponding wells. Mix well. The final volume is adjusted based on your siRNA concentration requirements.

Incubate and if possible, change to normal cell line media after 24 hours. Incubate the cells for 24–48 hours at 37°C in a CO2 incubator until you're ready to assay for gene knockdown.
