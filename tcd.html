Trinity College Dublin (TCD) Lab Equipment

Below is a list of the equipment available at Trinity College Dublin (TCD). These machines are located across various laboratories and can be used by members of the academic and research community. For more information or to reserve any equipment, please contact us at tcd-contact@example.com.

General Lab Equipment

  • Centrifuge – For separating components of a sample based on density.
  • Autoclave – Sterilizes laboratory equipment and media using high-pressure steam.
  • pH Meter – Measures the acidity or alkalinity of solutions in the lab.

Tissue Culture

  • Incubator (CO2) – For maintaining optimal cell culture environments for cancer cell lines.
  • Laminar Flow Hood – Ensures a sterile environment for cell culture work, critical for cancer research.
  • Cell Counter – Used for counting and analyzing cancer cell populations.

Genomics

  • Next-Generation Sequencer (NGS) – For high-throughput sequencing to study genetic mutations in cancer.
  • RT-PCR System – Used for analyzing gene expression levels in cancer research.
  • Microarray Scanner – For genome-wide analysis of gene expression in cancer cells.

Proteomics

  • Mass Spectrometer – For analyzing proteins and metabolites involved in cancer biology.
  • Western Blotting System – For protein analysis in cancer research using immunoblotting techniques.
  • Immunoassay Reader – Measures protein concentration in cancer-related experiments through ELISA.

Microscopy

  • Confocal Microscope – Allows high-resolution imaging of live cancer cell specimens.
  • Electron Microscope – For imaging cancer tissue samples at the nanometer scale.
  • Fluorescence Microscope – Used for examining cancer cells labeled with fluorescent markers.
