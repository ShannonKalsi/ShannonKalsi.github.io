Royal College of Surgeons in Ireland (RCSI) Lab Equipment

Below is a list of the equipment available in the Royal College of Surgeons in Ireland (RCSI). These machines are located within the research buildings and can be used by faculty, students, and researchers. For inquiries about using these equipment or to book time slots, please contact us at rcsi-contact@example.com.

Cancer Cells/Tissue Culture

  • Incubator (CO2) – For maintaining optimal cell culture environments.
  • Laminar Flow Hood – Ensures a sterile environment for cell culture work.
  • Cell Counter – Used for counting and analyzing cell populations.

Proteomics

  • Mass Spectrometer – For analyzing proteins and metabolites.
  • HPLC System – High-performance liquid chromatography for protein and peptide separation.
  • Western Blotting System – For protein analysis through immunoblotting techniques.
  • Immunoassay Reader – Measures protein concentration through enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay (ELISA).
  • GeoMX Digital Spatial Profiler – A high-performance imaging system for spatial profiling of tissue samples.

Microscopy

  • Confocal Microscope – Allows high-resolution imaging of live cell specimens.
  • Electron Microscope – For imaging biological and inorganic materials at the nanometer scale.
  • Fluorescence Microscope – Used for examining samples labeled with fluorescent markers.

General Lab Equipment

  • Centrifuge – For separating components of a sample based on density.
  • Autoclave – Sterilizes laboratory equipment and media using high-pressure steam.
  • pH Meter – Measures the acidity or alkalinity of solutions in the lab.
