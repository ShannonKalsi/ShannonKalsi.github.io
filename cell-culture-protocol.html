Cell Culture Protocol

Introduction

Cell culture is a fundamental technique used in biological research to study cellular processes, drug development, and tissue engineering. Below is a detailed protocol to help you set up and maintain a successful cell culture environment.

Materials and Equipment

  • Laminar flow hood
  • Incubator set at 37°C with 5% CO2
  • Cell culture flasks
  • Growth medium (e.g., DMEM with 10% FBS)
  • Trypsin-EDTA solution
  • Pipettes and sterile tips
  • 70% ethanol
  • Personal protective equipment (gloves, lab coat, safety goggles)

Procedure

1. Preparing the Workspace

Before starting, ensure the workspace is clean and sterile. Wipe down surfaces with 70% ethanol and ensure all equipment is sterilized.

2. Thawing Cells

  • Quickly thaw frozen cells in a 37°C water bath.
  • Transfer the cells to a 15 mL conical tube with 10 mL of pre-warmed growth medium.
  • Centrifuge at 1000 rpm for 5 minutes and discard the supernatant.
  • Resuspend the cell pellet in fresh growth medium and transfer to a culture flask.

3. Subculturing

Subculture cells when they reach 70-80% confluency:

  • Aspirate spent medium and wash cells with sterile PBS.
  • Add trypsin-EDTA solution to detach cells and incubate at 37°C for 2-5 minutes.
  • Neutralize trypsin with growth medium and transfer the cell suspension to a centrifuge tube.
  • Centrifuge, resuspend the pellet, and plate cells at the desired density.

Tips and Notes

Note: Always handle cells gently to avoid mechanical stress. Regularly check for contamination and discard cultures if contamination is detected.
